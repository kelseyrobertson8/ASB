How to Help

Shop Smart

One way to get involved is to shop smart! Where and how you spend your money has an impact on the economy and the products that continue to be manufactured and distributed. It is extremely important to be conscious of the quantity and quality of products being purchased. The textile industry is one of the main contributors to pollution through their use of inks, dyes, and synthetic chemicals along with large amounts of water contaminated by these materials. Our society is stuck in a viscous cycle of mass consumerism and mass production. This pattern has caused a reduction in cost and quality which leads to higher consumption. A great way to reduce this cycle is to buy local, quality items or second hand to avoid supporting the mass production of goods.

Some options in Harrisonburg

Vine and Fig

Vine and Fig is located in Harrisonburg, VA. It is a program of the New Community Project (NCP), a faith-based nonprofit organization committed to the creation of sustainable systems that care for the earth, empower people most marginalized and impoverished, educate and inspire, and build the foundation for a nonviolent lifestyle. They work to create transformation at both the personal level and community level in three key areas that have the highest impact; food, transportation, and built environment.

Give Volunteers

Give volunteers are a community of students who volunteer in the Harrisonburg community as well as in communities in Nicaragua, Tanzania, Thailand, Laos, and Nepal. They are interested in entrepreneurship, sustainability, adventure, and travel. They actively work on asset based, environmentally minded projects in the Harrisonburg community and enjoy exploring the adventures within the Shenandoah Valley.

Visit Give's webpage!

Green Hummingbird

Green Hummingbird sells fair trade clothing and accessories made by artisans that are located anywhere from the Shenandoah Valley all the way to the mountains of Tibet. The Green Hummingbird values morality in the making of the goods they offer, which includes artisans being paid a living wage for their craft.
